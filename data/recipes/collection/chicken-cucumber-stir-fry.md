## Ingredients
- 2 lbs chicken tender breast, cut into small cubes
- 1 cucumber, quartered lengthwise then diced
- 4 slices ginger
- 2 cloves garlic, sliced
- 1 tbsp spicy sauce or bean paste
- ½ tbsp soy sauce
- 3g (½ tsp) cornstarch
- 3g (½ tsp) pepper
- 1 tsp peanut oil (or neutral oil)
- 2g (½ tsp) salt
- 2g (½ tsp) chicken bouillon powder (optional)

## Instructions
1. Cut chicken tender breast into small cubes.
2. Marinate chicken with pepper, cornstarch, and a little oil for 10 minutes.
3. Slice ginger and garlic. Cut cucumber into quarters lengthwise, then dice into small pieces.
4. Heat oil in a wok or large pan over medium-high heat. Add ginger and garlic, stir-fry until fragrant.
5. Add spicy sauce or bean paste and cook until oil turns reddish, about 1 minute.
6. Add chicken and stir-fry until it changes color and is cooked through, about 4-5 minutes.
7. Pour in soy sauce and toss to coat.
8. Add cucumber and stir briefly, just 1-2 minutes (don't overcook — keep it slightly crunchy).
9. Season with salt and bouillon if using. Serve immediately.

## Notes
- Don't overcook the cucumber — it should stay a little crisp
- Marinating the chicken keeps it tender
- Can sub bean paste for spicy sauce if you want less heat
