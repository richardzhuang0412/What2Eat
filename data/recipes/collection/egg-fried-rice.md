## Ingredients
- 1.5 cups cooked rice (day-old or cold rice works best)
- 2 eggs
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil (optional but recommended)
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 2 green onions, sliced (optional)
- Salt and white pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Make sure your rice is cold or at room temperature — freshly cooked rice gets mushy. If you just cooked it, spread it on a plate and let it cool for 10-15 min.
2. Heat your wok over high heat until it just starts to smoke. Add 1 tbsp of vegetable oil.
3. Crack in the eggs and scramble them quickly — you want soft, slightly underdone curds. Remove and set aside.
4. Add another tbsp of oil, then add the rice. Press it down and let it sit for 30 seconds to get a little crispy on the bottom.
5. Stir-fry the rice, breaking up any clumps, for about 2-3 minutes.
6. Pour in the soy sauce and toss everything together.
7. Add the scrambled eggs back in and mix through.
8. Finish with sesame oil and green onions if using. Season with salt and white pepper.
9. Plate it up and eat immediately!

## Notes
- Cold/day-old rice is key — it fries up much better than fresh rice
- High heat makes all the difference — get that wok screaming hot
- Customize with whatever you have: frozen peas, corn, a splash of fish sauce, garlic
- A little oyster sauce mixed in with the soy sauce takes it to the next level
